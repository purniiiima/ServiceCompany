Other Financial Products

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)

ETFs are investment funds traded on stock exchanges, holding assets such as stocks, commodities, or bonds.

  • Features: Liquidity, transparency, flexibility.
  • Benefits: Diversification, low expense ratios, intraday trading.
  • Investment Options: Equity ETFs, bond ETFs, commodity ETFs, sector ETFs.
Explore ETFs

Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs)

IRAs are tax-advantaged investment accounts designed to help individuals save for retirement.

  • Features: Tax benefits, investment options, flexibility.
  • Benefits: Tax-deferred or tax-free growth, wide range of investment choices.
  • Investment Options: Stocks, bonds, mutual funds, ETFs, CDs.
Explore IRAs

Certificate of Deposit (CD)

A Certificate of Deposit (CD) is a time deposit offered by banks and credit unions that pays a fixed interest rate for a specified term.

  • Features: Fixed interest rate, FDIC insurance (for bank CDs), maturity date.
  • Benefits: Low risk, predictable returns, FDIC insurance (for bank CDs).
  • Investment Options: Short-term CDs, long-term CDs, jumbo CDs.
Explore CDs

401(k) Plans

A 401(k) plan is a retirement savings plan sponsored by an employer that allows employees to save and invest a portion of their paycheck.

  • Features: Employer match (optional), tax-deferred growth, contribution limits.
  • Benefits: Tax advantages, automatic contributions, potential for employer matching.
  • Investment Options: Mutual funds, ETFs, target-date funds, company stock.
Explore 401(k) Plans

Savings Accounts

A savings account is a basic deposit account offered by banks and credit unions that allows individuals to deposit money and earn interest.

  • Features: FDIC insurance (for bank savings accounts), easy access to funds, interest earnings.
  • Benefits: Safety of principal, liquidity, no investment risk.
  • Investment Options: High-yield savings accounts, money market accounts.
Explore Savings Accounts
